Channel attribution
Source: traffic.session__dim_growth_channel joined to traffic.session__fct_sessions

Model: last-touch, click-based, pre-order sessions only. Each order is attributed to the single most recent session before the order was placed, matched on id_user_first.

This analysis uses DIRECT_LANDING channel only — the largest channel by volume (~72% of all partner orders). Direct landing is the expected capture point for users influenced by brand and upper-funnel media (TV, OTT, YouTube) who return directly rather than clicking through a tracked link.

Organic baseline methodology
Airport Transfer
Linear regression fitted on Jan 31–Apr 25 (13 pre-launch weeks). Airport was on a strong, consistent organic growth curve before media launched — extending that trendline forward gives a conservative upper bound on what would have happened without media. Any orders above this line post-launch are treated as incremental.

The generous baseline intentionally avoids overclaiming media credit given how steep the pre-existing growth was.
Grocery (Instacart)
Flat average of Apr 11–Apr 25 (~1,968 orders/wk). Grocery peaked in late March (~2,813) then pulled back sharply through mid-April before stabilizing. Rather than extrapolating a downtrend that may not have continued, the flat average of the three pre-launch weeks is used as the most defensible "holding pattern" baseline.

The alt baseline (Apr 11–25 regression) projects the downtrend forward and is shown for comparison — it gives media more credit but assumes the decline was structural rather than temporary.
Key caveats
Attribution model limitations Last-touch click-based attribution undercounts brand and upper-funnel channels (TV, OTT, YouTube) which drive awareness but not direct clicks. DIRECT_LANDING is the proxy for this influence but cannot isolate it precisely.
Session filter window Session data filtered from Jan 25, 2026. Orders where the user's last session predates Jan 25 are excluded from the attribution query entirely — this may cause a small undercount in early weeks.
Incrementality is estimated These are observational estimates, not controlled experiments. No holdout group was run. True incrementality would require a geo or user-level test. Treat CPB figures as directional, not precise.
Spend completeness Spend data covers Apr 26–May 31 only. Pre-launch OLV spend for Airport Transfer (Apr 13–27) is included but may not capture all impression-based activity prior to the main campaign launch.
