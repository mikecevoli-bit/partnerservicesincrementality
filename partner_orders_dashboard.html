Airport Transfer
Media appears to be driving lift above where the product was naturally heading. Orders tracked spend activity with a ~1-week lag — when spend pulled back in late May, orders followed the next week; when spend returned in June, orders stabilized.

Most-likely cost per booking: $98–$125. Confidence is moderate — strong organic growth before launch makes it harder to isolate media's exact contribution.
Grocery (Instacart)
Strongest media signal of the two products. Impression volume explains 89% of week-to-week variation in orders above baseline. Grocery was recovering from an April dip when media launched, so some caution around media-driven vs. natural recovery.

Most-likely cost per booking: $174–$216. Confidence is strong on the correlation; the baseline assumption is the main source of uncertainty.

Why it was built
Partner product bookings (Airport Transfer, Grocery) happen outside Airbnb's standard tracking environment. The partner passes back matched orders to Airbnb user IDs, but only about 28% of those events have a traceable last-touch path — meaning most bookings can't be tied to a specific ad click or channel.

For the remaining ~72%, the last known touchpoint is a direct session to Airbnb (DIRECT_LANDING). Brand media — TV, OTT, YouTube, social — typically drives awareness rather than direct clicks, so users influenced by it tend to return directly rather than through a tracked link. This makes DIRECT_LANDING the best available proxy for brand media impact, though it's not a guaranteed attribution.

An integrated solution is currently in progress to connect partner conversion data directly with Airbnb's backend attribution infrastructure. When live, this will replace the proxy approach with true conversion-level attribution and significantly improve measurement fidelity for these products.
What it's for
Use this as a directional read on whether brand media is making an impact — not as a precise measurement. It's useful for:
  • Confirming media activity is correlated with order lift above organic trend
  • Establishing a rough cost-per-booking range for planning and investment conversations
  • Tracking whether that relationship holds or changes over time as a baseline
What it's not for
  • Forecasting or projecting future bookings
  • Accounting for macro factors — product changes, seasonality, competitive shifts, or external demand drivers are not in the model
  • Precise ROI reporting — significant changes to spend levels, product experience, or front-end efficiency would require the model to be re-run to remain valid

Organic baseline methodology
Airport Transfer — slope blend
Average of full Jan–Apr slope (~298/wk) and Apr 4–25 slope (~108/wk), anchored at 4/25 actual (3,694). Result: ~203 orders/week. Reflects long-run growth and April deceleration signal. Alt baseline: full Jan–Apr regression.
Grocery — flat Apr 11–25 average
Flat average of Apr 11–Apr 25 (~1,968 orders/wk). Reflects stabilized demand before launch rather than projecting a temporary April dip forward as a continuing decline. Alt: Apr 11–25 regression.
Key caveats
Attribution modelLast-touch click model undercounts brand/upper-funnel channels. DIRECT_LANDING is a proxy, not a direct measurement.
Incrementality is estimatedNo holdout group was run. CPB figures are directional, not precise ROI measurements.
Impressions vs. spendImpressions reflect actual exposure volume. Spend varies with CPM shifts across channels — equal spend ≠ equal reach.
Week conventionAll data uses week-ending Saturday (Sun–Sat). Orders from dim_time; media from weekly export.
