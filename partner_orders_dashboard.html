Airport Transfer
Media drove clear lift through mid-June. Orders tracked spend through 6/6, then surged 6/13–6/27 beyond what media impressions alone explain — likely amplified by FIFA World Cup demand. The impression-order correlation is weak over the full 9 weeks (r=0.16) because the June surge inflates organic.

Most-likely cost per booking: $179–$215 (IQR, excl. ramp-up week). Confidence is Weak on correlation; the baseline trajectory is the more reliable signal here.
Grocery (Instacart)
Impression volume explains 57% of week-to-week variation in orders above baseline (r=0.76, p=0.02). The correlation is Moderate-Strong and has held across all 9 post-launch weeks. Grocery order volume is tracking media reach closely, with no obvious confounding external demand spike.

Most-likely cost per booking: $113–$154 (IQR, excl. ramp-up week). Confidence Moderate-Strong.

Why it was built
Partner product bookings (Airport Transfer, Grocery) happen outside Airbnb's standard tracking environment. The partner passes back matched orders to Airbnb user IDs, but only about 28% of those events have a traceable last-touch path — meaning most bookings can't be tied to a specific ad click or channel.

For the remaining ~72%, DIRECT_LANDING + BRAND_SOCIAL are used as a proxy for brand media impact. Brand media drives awareness, not direct clicks, so influenced users return directly rather than through a tracked link.

An integrated attribution solution is in progress. When live, it will replace this proxy approach with true conversion-level measurement.
What it's for
Use as a directional read on whether brand media correlates with order lift. Useful for confirming media-order correlation, establishing rough CPB benchmarks, and tracking the relationship over time.
What it's not for
Forecasting, accounting for external demand factors (World Cup, seasonality, product changes), or precise ROI reporting. Significant shifts in spend, product experience, or external events require a model refresh.

Organic baseline methodology
Airport Transfer — slope blend
Average of full Jan–Apr slope (~253/wk) and Apr 4–25 slope (~109/wk), anchored at 4/25 actual (3,698). Result: ~181 orders/week. Reflects long-run growth and April deceleration signal. Alt baseline: full Jan–Apr regression.
Grocery — flat Apr 11–25 average
Flat average of Apr 11–Apr 25 (~1,974 orders/wk). Reflects stabilized demand before launch rather than projecting a temporary April dip forward as a continuing decline. Alt: Apr 11–25 regression.
Key caveats
External demand (FIFA World Cup)Airport orders surged 6/13–6/27 beyond what media impressions predict. World Cup travel demand likely boosted organic airport bookings independently of media spend, diluting the measured correlation.
Incrementality is estimatedNo holdout group was run. CPB figures are directional, not precise ROI measurements.
Impressions vs. spendImpressions reflect actual exposure volume. Spend varies with CPM shifts — equal spend ≠ equal reach.
