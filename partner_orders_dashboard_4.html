Organic baseline methodology
Airport Transfer — Slope blend
Hybrid baseline averaging the full Jan–Apr regression slope (~298/wk) with the Apr 4–25 slope (~108/wk), anchored at the last pre-launch data point (3,694 on 4/25). Resulting slope: ~203 orders/week.

Rationale: Airport Transfer growth materially decelerated in April (avg +112 orders/wk vs. +298 over the full period), suggesting the product was approaching a natural plateau. The blend honors the long-run trend while acknowledging the slowdown immediately before launch.
Grocery — Flat Apr 11–25 average
Flat average of Apr 11–Apr 25 (~1,968 orders/wk). Grocery had a sharp pullback in April after a March peak — holding flat reflects the stabilized run rate before launch rather than projecting a temporary dip forward as a continuing decline.
Key caveats
Attribution modelLast-touch click model undercounts brand/upper-funnel channels. DIRECT_LANDING is a proxy, not a direct measurement.
Incrementality is estimatedNo holdout group was run. CPB figures are directional, not precise ROI measurements.
Spend–order lagAirport Transfer shows a ~1-week lag between spend changes and order volume changes. The 5/30 and 6/6 order declines track the spend pullback on 5/23 and 5/30. This lag is evidence of media dependency beyond what the baseline incremental math captures.
Week conventionAll data uses week-ending Saturday (Sun–Sat). Orders from dim_time ds_week_ending; media from weekly aggregated export.
